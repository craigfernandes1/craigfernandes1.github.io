About me

Hi, I'm Craig! I'm a fourth-year Operations Research PhD Candidate and Vanier Scholar at the University of Toronto, co-advised by Professors Timothy Chan and Ningyuan Chen.

My research integrates operations management, market design, and analytics to study innovative markets that require tailored models and analyses. A central theme in my work is understanding and improving how individuals participate and perform in these uncertain environments. For example, I have designed income-sharing contracts to manage risk in superstar labor markets, mechanisms to incentivize reviewing effort in academic conferences, pricing strategies that account for bargaining in B2B transactions, and decision-making tools for both professional and recreational sports. Across these settings, I work closely with data to ensure that theoretical insights translate into practical policies.

I was a visiting researcher at the Tuck School of Business (Dartmouth College) in Summer 2024 and previously worked as a Data Scientist on Amazon's SCOT team in Summer 2021.

I'm on the 2025-26 academic job market!
